Biography

I am a researcher working on experimental quantum physics with trapped ions at the University of Innsbruck.

We are researching several techniques that could be the basis for a future quantum computer. We use quantum registers consisting of strings of individual atoms that store information in their internal state. We trap ionized Calcium atoms and manipulate their state with laser pulses. Below is a short movie of a quantum register with 55 ions from our AQTION project.

We are now adapting the techniques we develop for quantum information processing towards controlling single molecules and learn about their structure and dynamics.

Below is a short description of my research interests:

Quantum algorithms

We are researching the building blocks and architecture of future quantum computers based on trapped atomic ions. In our experiments, we trap single atomic ions in vacuum and manipulate them with laser light. Each single atom stores a single qubit, the smallest unit of quantum information. We create exotic entangled quantum states and perform small-scale quantum algorithms in quantum registers with more than 10 qubits… More

Scalable quantum architectures

We use the knowledge of implementing algorithms as the basis for developing quantum computing architectures that will be capable of controlling more than hundred qubits. In collaboration with Infineon technologies, we are fabricating and testing the building blocks of such an architecture in an industrial setting… More

Quantum control of single molecules

Molecules show a much richer internal structure than atoms and offer thus complex quantum dynamics that are not fully undestood. Within the QCosmo project, we will use the control techniques that we have developed for single atomic ions to characterize and control ultrafast dynamics in single molecular ions… More

Growing up in the Tyrolean alps has taught me appreciation for the nature around us, which I wholeheartedly enjoy.
